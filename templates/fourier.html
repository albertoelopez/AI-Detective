What is the Fourier Transform?

The Fourier Transform is a mathematical tool that decomposes a signal into its constituent frequencies. Think of it like a prism splitting white light into a rainbow - it reveals the "ingredients" that make up a signal.

For images: It shows which spatial patterns (edges, textures, gradients) exist.
For audio: It shows which sound frequencies (bass, treble, harmonics) are present.
For video: It can analyze both spatial (per-frame) and temporal (over time) patterns.

AI-generated content often has telltale frequency signatures that differ from natural content. This lab lets you explore these patterns hands-on.

How 2D FFT Works on Images

A 2D FFT converts an image from the spatial domain (pixels arranged in rows/columns) to the frequency domain (which patterns exist at different scales).

  • Low frequencies (center of FFT): Large-scale features like overall brightness, gradients
  • High frequencies (edges of FFT): Fine details like edges, textures, noise
  • Horizontal/Vertical lines in FFT: Correspond to vertical/horizontal patterns in image

How FFT Works on Audio

Audio FFT reveals which frequencies are present in a sound. For speech and music, this shows fundamental frequencies and their harmonics (overtones).

  • Spectrogram: Shows how frequencies change over time (x=time, y=frequency, color=intensity)
  • Harmonics: Natural sounds have integer-multiple overtones; AI may have irregular patterns
  • Mel scale: Frequencies mapped to human perception (we hear differences better at low frequencies)

How FFT Works on Video

Video has two dimensions for FFT analysis: spatial (within each frame) and temporal (across frames over time).

  • Spatial FFT: Same as image FFT, applied to each frame
  • Temporal FFT: Analyzes how pixels change over time - reveals motion patterns, flickering
  • Deepfake artifacts: Often visible as temporal inconsistencies, especially around manipulated faces
