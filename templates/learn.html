Why Learn These Techniques?

AI-generated content leaves behind subtle traces that differ from naturally captured media. By understanding signal processing, image forensics, and pattern analysis, you can learn to identify these artifacts. Each technique reveals different aspects of how content was created.

Frequency Domain Analysis

~
Fourier Transform (FFT)
Images, Audio, Video

Decompose signals into frequency components. Reveals periodic artifacts, missing high-frequency detail, and unnatural frequency distributions common in AI content.

Beginner Foundation technique
W
Wavelet Transform
Images, Audio

Like FFT but with spatial/temporal localization. Shows exactly WHERE frequency artifacts occur, making it easier to spot localized manipulations and AI artifacts.

Intermediate Multi-resolution
C
Cepstral Analysis (MFCCs)
Audio

The "spectrum of a spectrum" - reveals voice characteristics and speech patterns. Synthetic voices have different cepstral fingerprints than human voices.

Intermediate Voice analysis

Image Forensics

E
Error Level Analysis (ELA)
Images

Analyzes JPEG compression artifacts to detect edits. Different regions compress differently based on their history - manipulated areas stand out visually.

Beginner Very visual
N
Noise Analysis
Images

Camera sensors produce characteristic noise patterns. AI-generated images have synthetic or absent noise, revealing their artificial origin.

Intermediate Sensor forensics
H
Histogram Analysis
Images

Examine color and brightness distributions. AI images often have unnatural peaks, gaps, or overly smooth histograms that differ from camera-captured images.

Beginner Color analysis

Pattern & Structure Analysis

G
Gradient & Edge Analysis
Images, Video

Analyze edge sharpness and gradient patterns. AI often produces edges that are too smooth, too sharp, or have inconsistent gradients compared to real photos.

Beginner Edge detection
A
Autocorrelation
Images, Audio

Detect repetitive patterns by correlating a signal with itself. Reveals copy-paste artifacts, looping, and periodic structures from AI generation.

Intermediate Repetition detection
O
Optical Flow
Video

Track motion between video frames. Deepfakes often have unnatural motion patterns, especially around manipulated faces where optical flow becomes inconsistent.

Advanced Motion analysis

Key Concepts

Time/Space vs Frequency Domain

Signals can be viewed as values over time/space OR as combinations of frequencies. Different artifacts are visible in each domain.

Compression Artifacts

JPEG, MP3, and video codecs leave traces. AI content often has different compression histories than claimed.

Statistical Consistency

Natural media has consistent statistical properties. AI often has subtle inconsistencies across regions or time.

Sensor Characteristics

Cameras and microphones have unique signatures. AI-generated content lacks these physical device traces.
